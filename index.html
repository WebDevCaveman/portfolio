The Forge

Now the doors.

Chamber I · MSI Polska

A team of twenty, a month of work. Then: five people, one week.

Hardware reviews came in as claims - screenshots, view counts, click reports - and lived in email threads and spreadsheets. I moved the whole trade onto Airtable, Bubble, and Make.

Forms that make the wrong entry hard to submit. Folder structures that build themselves on Google Drive. Presentations that assemble in Google Slides and go straight to a manager for approval.

Six months of work together. The same output, at a fifth of the cost in hands and a quarter of the time.

Chamber II · RadarPremier

A hall where the work is set out and judged.

Built for easycart - a place where sellers using the cart could put a product forward and let it be seen.

Upvotes and a running ranking - daily and weekly. Product pages, ratings, comments. Seller profiles. And a launch, so a new thing arrives with a bit of noise.

Chamber III · ARCHICOM

Two workbenches, one vein of ore.

Both benches draw from the same seam: Webcon, the system this developer already ran on. Sound underneath, unlovely on top, and priced per head - so every new user cost more. I built the face; Webcon kept the records.

Tenders

A dashboard for subcontractors: open and closed tenders, submissions approved by the developer's own department, and each bid walked through its steps - entry, pricing, documents, outcome, contract.

A knowledge base and a channel to ask took repeat questions down by sixty-five percent.

Sales

A tablet portal for the seller standing on site. Clickable plans - buildings, floors, apartments - each one marked free, reserved, or sold. Live prices and statuses drawn from Webcon.

Offers that report back when the client opens them, that can be reserved only from within, and that expire when they should.

That's the work nobody photographs: making a friendly thing sit on top of a rigid one, and keeping the two in step.

Chamber IV · StudentSpace

A house that fills itself a year ahead.

Webflow at the front - fast, findable, ready for ads. Bubble behind it, doing the booking. The same seam underneath: Webcon, holding the records.

Rooms reserved a full academic year early. Accounts for an adult student or a legal guardian. Two-person rooms where you bring someone or are matched by gender. A dashboard for contracts, moving in, moving out, extending, changing rooms. A noticeboard for the whole hall, your building, or just your floor. And the air conditioning in your own room, turned from your own screen.

Chamber V · Two MVPs

Rebuilding familiar worlds from nothing.

Job board

Search and filters by location, industry, and type. Listing pages, company profiles, candidate accounts with a CV, applying in a single click, saved roles, and a panel for the employer.

Luxury car rental

Clients, cars, and the days each is free. Product pages with the specs that matter. A client dashboard for balance, points, history, and details; an admin dashboard behind it. Booking against a live availability calendar, each rental tracked through its statuses.

Chamber VI · CoralCare

Joining a crew already at work.

Slack, Jira, daily stand-ups - a team mid-stride, and a place to fit into it. I came in on QA and took over a developer's work when he left.

Testing my own build and other people's, straight alongside the client, in a stack that reached well past Bubble.

The project has since moved on to code - the natural next step once a thing has proven itself.
